Language learning is not primarily about intelligence or talent. It is about consistency. A student who reviews for twenty minutes every day will, over a year, far outpace a student who studies for three hours every Sunday and nothing in between.

The question is not *how much* to study, it is *how to make study a daily habit*.

## 1. Review Vocabulary Every Morning

Before you open anything else, spend five to ten minutes reviewing vocabulary from your last class. The spacing effect is real: material reviewed shortly after learning, then again at increasing intervals, is retained far more effectively than material crammed in long sessions.

A simple flashcard system, physical cards or a free app, is sufficient.

## 2. Read Aloud Daily

Even five minutes of reading Arabic aloud, without full comprehension, trains your ear and your tongue. Arabic phonology is different enough from English that regular oral practice is necessary to make the sounds automatic.

Choose a short passage you have already studied. Read it slowly. Read it again.

## 3. Write Out What You Have Memorised

Writing reinforces memory in a way that reading does not. After learning a new grammatical pattern, write out five sentences using it. After learning new vocabulary, write the words in context.

This does not need to be long. Ten minutes of focused writing is valuable.

## 4. Return to What You Have Already Studied

New students often want to move forward. Experienced students know that returning to earlier material and finding it newly clear is one of the most satisfying experiences in language learning, and one of the most productive.

Once a week, return to a lesson from two or three weeks ago. You will notice things you missed.

## 5. Make Du'a in Arabic

If your motivation for learning Classical Arabic is spiritual, use it spiritually. Begin by learning one or two supplications (*du'a*) from the Qur'an or Sunnah and understand their grammar. Recite them with awareness.

This connects the academic work to its purpose, and purpose is the most powerful motivator of all.
